# v1.5.2 — vpad polish + iOS page-lock

Per-iPhone feedback from v1.5.1: vpad was sitting too high and too visible, and swiping down on the board would also drag the whole Safari viewport. Fixed both.

## Changed

### Vpad reads as a near-invisible hint at the true bottom

- Default opacity **0.18 → 0.08** — still discoverable, no longer competing with the board.
- Centered with `left: 50%; transform: translateX(-50%)` and capped at `min(360px, 100vw - 16px)` so it occupies a thumb-reach footprint instead of a full-width strip.
- Button heights tightened to `clamp(38px, 6dvh, 50px)` so the pad doesn't eat vertical space when revealed.

## Fixed

### Page no longer rubber-band-scrolls when swiping down on the board

Three changes work together so iOS Safari stops fighting our gesture handler:

1. **`touch-action: none` on `.board-frame`** — tells iOS we own every gesture on the play surface.
2. **Touch listeners switched to `{ passive: false }`** — the v1.5.0 listeners were passive, so `e.preventDefault()` was silently ignored. They now block iOS's default pan/scroll on every single-finger move.
3. **`html, body` get `position: fixed; overflow: hidden; touch-action: none`** on mobile — removes the document from the scroll container entirely, killing pull-to-refresh and the rubber-band overscroll at the root.

The board now stays locked under your finger during a soft-drop drag, the whole way down.
